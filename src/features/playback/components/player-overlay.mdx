## Design philosophy

The Now Playing screen is where Fermata's vision statement lives or dies. Every design decision answers one question: *does this help someone sit down and joyfully listen to music?*

<blockquote style={{
  borderLeft: '3px solid #D4A0FF',
  paddingLeft: 20,
  margin: '24px 0',
  fontStyle: 'italic',
  fontSize: 16,
  color: '#9898A3',
}}>
  The app should feel like a quiet room with good speakers — warm, intentional, nothing competing for attention.
</blockquote>

## States

### Paused

<Canvas of={PlayerOverlayStories.Paused} />

When paused, the vinyl disc stops rotating with a gentle deceleration (800ms ease-out, +15 degrees) — like the platter slowing after you lift the needle.

## Mini player

<Canvas of={PlayerOverlayStories.MiniPlayer} />

The mini player sits above the tab bar. A thin accent-colored progress line runs across the very top — always visible, never distracting. Tapping anywhere expands to the full player.

## Why vinyl?

<div style={{ padding: 20, backgroundColor: '#141416', borderRadius: 12, margin: '24px 0' }}>
  <p style={{ fontSize: 14, color: '#9898A3', lineHeight: 1.8, margin: 0 }}>
    The circular artwork with center hole isn't nostalgia — it's a <strong style={{ color: '#E8E8ED' }}>commitment device</strong>.
    A spinning disc says "this is playing, right now, and you chose it." It's the opposite of a progress bar ticking
    in the background while you do something else. The rotation catches your eye when you glance at your phone.
    The deceleration when you pause feels like a physical act. It turns playback into presence.
  </p>
</div>
