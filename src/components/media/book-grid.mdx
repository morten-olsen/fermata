## Design rationale

The BookGrid is the audiobooks tab's equivalent of AlbumGrid. The key difference: **3 columns of portrait cards** versus 2 columns of square cards. This creates a bookshelf aesthetic that immediately communicates "these are books."

<div style={{ display: 'grid', gridTemplateColumns: '1fr 1fr', gap: 16, margin: '24px 0' }}>
  <div style={{ padding: 16, backgroundColor: '#141416', borderRadius: 12 }}>
    <p style={{ fontWeight: 600, color: '#D4A0FF', marginBottom: 4 }}>3 columns</p>
    <p style={{ fontSize: 13, color: '#9898A3' }}>Portrait cards are narrower than square cards, so 3 fit comfortably. This also means more books visible per screen — appropriate for audiobook libraries which tend to be browsed, not scrolled.</p>
  </div>
  <div style={{ padding: 16, backgroundColor: '#141416', borderRadius: 12 }}>
    <p style={{ fontWeight: 600, color: '#D4A0FF', marginBottom: 4 }}>Progress-aware</p>
    <p style={{ fontSize: 13, color: '#9898A3' }}>Accepts a <code style={{ color: '#D4A0FF' }}>progressMap</code> prop that passes per-book progress to each <code style={{ color: '#D4A0FF' }}>BookCard</code>. Progress badges appear on in-progress books.</p>
  </div>
